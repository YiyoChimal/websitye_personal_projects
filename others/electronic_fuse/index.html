Analog Electoronic Fuse

Objective

Design and develop an analog electronic fuse capable of monitoring voltage, current, and temperature to provide fast and reliable protection for DC electronic systems against abnormal operating conditions.

Technologies Used

• KiCad – Electronic schematic capture and PCB design.

• PROTEUS – Circuit simulation and design validation.

• SolidWorks – Mechanical design of the device enclosure.

My Contributions

• Designed the electronic schematic and custom PCB using KiCad.

• Simulated and validated the analog protection circuit using PROTEUS before PCB fabrication.

• Performed physical testing to verify circuit performance.

• Designed a compact protective enclosure using SolidWorks.

• Integrated the electronic and mechanical components into a complete protection device.

Technical Highlights

• Fully analog electronic protection system.

• Custom-designed PCB.

• Real-time monitoring of voltage, current, and temperature.

• Operating range: 0–120 VDC.

• Current protection up to 6 A.

• Temperature monitoring up to 150°C.

• Internal resistance of only 0.1 Ω.

• Maximum protection cutoff time of 10 ms.

Results

Successfully developed a compact analog electronic fuse capable of protecting DC systems operating from 0–120 VDC, with current protection up to 6 A and temperature monitoring up to 150°C. The device achieved a low internal resistance of 0.1 Ω and a maximum cutoff time of 10 ms, providing fast and efficient protection for electronic equipment.
