Air humidity condenser

Objective

Design and develop an alternative water condensation system that extracts moisture from ambient air using thermoelectric Peltier cells. The system was designed to operate without refrigerant gases while maintaining low energy consumption through precise temperature control.

Technologies Used

• SolidWorks – Mechanical design and 3D modeling.

• KiCad – Electronic schematic capture and PCB design.

• C – Embedded firmware development.

• ATmega328P – Main embedded controller.

• PID Control – Closed-loop temperature regulation.

My Contributions

• Designed the mechanical structure using SolidWorks.

• Designed the electronic schematic and custom PCB using KiCad.

• Programmed the ATmega328P microcontroller in Embedded C.

• Implemented a PID-based temperature control algorithm for efficient condensation.

• Integrated the mechanical, electronic, and embedded control systems into a complete prototype.

Technical Highlights

• Thermoelectric cooling using Peltier cells.

• Custom-designed PCB.

• ATmega328P-based embedded controller.

• PID closed-loop temperature control.

• Refrigerant-free water condensation system.

• Low-power and energy-efficient operation.

• Modular mechanical design.

Results

Successfully developed a refrigeration-free atmospheric water condensation system capable of producing approximately 200 mL of water per day under environmental conditions of 60% relative humidity and 23°C. The system operates without refrigerant gases and incorporates a PID-controlled thermal management system to maximize condensation efficiency while maintaining low energy consumption.
