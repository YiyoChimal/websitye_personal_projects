NASA Space Apps Challenge 2023 – Community-Based Fire Management

Objective

Design and develop a low-cost communication device capable of transmitting wildfire alerts to remote communities with limited or no access to conventional communication infrastructure. The system was designed to provide a reliable, low-power, and long-range solution for emergency communication during wildfire events.

Technologies Used

• KiCad – Electronic schematic capture and PCB design.

• LoRa – Long-range, low-power wireless communication.

• SolidWorks – Mechanical design of the device enclosure.

My Contributions

• Designed the electronic schematic and custom PCB using KiCad.

• Implemented the LoRa wireless communication system for long-range, low-power data transmission.

• Designed a rugged enclosure using SolidWorks for outdoor deployment.

• Contributed to the integration of the electronic, mechanical, and communication subsystems into a functional prototype.

Technical Highlights

• Solar-powered autonomous operation.

• LoRa-based peer-to-peer wireless communication.

• Communication range of up to 3 km.

• Low-power electronic design for extended field operation.

• Custom-designed PCB and rugged enclosure.

• Designed for remote and infrastructure-limited environments.

Results

Successfully developed a solar-powered, low-energy communication device capable of transmitting wildfire alerts over distances of up to 3 km using LoRa technology. The project was awarded 1st Place at the regional NASA Space Apps Challenge 2023 in Xalapa, Veracruz, Mexico, and was recognized as a Global Nominee, ranking among the top 500 projects worldwide. The project demonstrated the ability to develop practical engineering solutions that address real-world environmental and social challenges.
